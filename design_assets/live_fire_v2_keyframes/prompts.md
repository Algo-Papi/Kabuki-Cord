# Live-fire Mode V2 Sprite Prompt

CLI fallback image generation used `gpt-image-2` at high quality. The accepted default scanner V2 atlas controlled the Kabuki actor identity, mask markings, costume, proportions, rendering, palette, camera, and scale. The original Live-fire sheet supplied the action theme, while its clearest sweep frame supplied only the opaque white-yellow, orange, and deep-red pixel-painted flame language.

The final brief requested exactly eight cells in a 4x2 atlas. It locked one continuous motion and flame system: grounded anticipation with one palm ember; compact hand and sole ignition; sole-powered short lift; one hand-anchored crescent sweep; narrowing arc during descent; three-point landing with a shallow contact flare; rising extinguish to one fingertip ember; stable recovery with only three fading motes.

The prompt required one fixed camera and scale, a modest physically explained lift, one continuous up-and-down path, one-directional arc progression, return to the original ground line, and a visibly extinguished final frame. It prohibited weapons, props, duplicate limbs, detached hands, full-body flame rings, flaming eyes, giant fireballs, explosions, smoke, ash clouds, environmental fire, text, random glyphs, logos, watermarks, and any variation in the flat `#00ff00` chroma-key background.

The CLI source is `output/imagegen/live-fire-v2-atlas-chromakey.png`, copied into the project as `design_assets/live-fire-v2-atlas-chromakey.png`. The alpha-extracted source is `design_assets/live-fire-v2-atlas-transparent.png`.
