# Delivery Celebration V2 Sprite Prompt

CLI fallback image generation used `gpt-image-2` at high quality. The accepted default scanner V2 atlas controlled the Kabuki actor identity, mask markings, costume, proportions, rendering, palette, camera, and scale. The original Arigato actor supplied only the restrained friendly thumbs-up conclusion, while the accepted Posting V2 atlas supplied the cream paper, red seal, and cyan confirmation-accent design language.

The final brief requested exactly eight cells in a 4x2 atlas. It locked one delivery-confirmation receipt throughout this sequence: guarded anticipation; notice one incoming receipt; catch it with both hands; inspect its fixed seal and cyan accent with one golden glint; perform one compact acknowledgement bow; tuck the same receipt into one inner robe fold; raise one friendly thumbs-up with three small stars; settle while those stars become three faint motes.

The prompt required one fixed camera, actor scale, ground line, receipt size, cream color, red seal, cyan accent, and connected hand contact. It prohibited duplicate paper, mailboxes, readable writing, speech bubbles, confetti clouds, giant starbursts, fireworks, money, gifts, weapons, duplicate limbs, random glyphs, logos, watermarks, and any variation in the flat `#00ff00` chroma-key background.

The CLI source is `output/imagegen/delivery-celebration-v2-atlas-chromakey.png`, copied into the project as `design_assets/delivery-celebration-v2-atlas-chromakey.png`. The alpha-extracted source is `design_assets/delivery-celebration-v2-atlas-transparent.png`.
